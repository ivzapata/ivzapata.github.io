I am a PhD student in the Department of Political Science at Texas Tech University,
and a Research Assistant at the [Free Market Institute](https://www.depts.ttu.edu/freemarketinstitute/). I hold a B.A. in Law from the
Pontificia Universidad Javeriana in Colombia and an M.A. in Business Law from the
University of Mannheim in Germany.

My research lies at the intersection of comparative and international political
economy. I study the political and institutional foundations of economic development,
with particular attention to how governments in weak-institutional settings build
credibility, constrain themselves, and shape the behavior of economic actors. Most of
my work centers on Latin America and uses quasi-experimental methods to study how
these dynamics play out in practice.
